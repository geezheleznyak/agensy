# Principles and Postulates of the [vault name] Project

*Rename this file to the vault's specific name if desired, e.g., `principles-and-postulates.md` (generic), `commander-postulates.md` (training domain-specific).*

These postulates are the non-negotiable load-bearing walls of this vault. Every note, map, and curriculum phase must be consistent with them. **They are not conclusions of the inquiry — they are the prior commitments that structure it.** Where a source or concept conflicts with a postulate, that conflict must be named and examined, not suppressed.

**Status progression**: `awaiting-user-fill` → `seeded-v0.1` (3–5 postulates articulated) → `seeded-v1.0` (the stable set — typically 5–10 postulates, rarely more). The vault cannot begin producing serious content until at least `seeded-v0.1` — curriculum phases drift into topic-coverage without postulates to teach toward.

**Distinction from `writer-positions.md` (expression vaults)**: postulates constrain what the vault *teaches*; positions constrain what the vault's author *argues*. A training vault teaches toward its postulates; an expression vault argues from its positions. The overlap is real but the direction is different.

---

## How to identify postulates

A postulate is a claim the vault takes as *prior* — a premise that structures everything the vault does. Tests for whether a candidate is really a postulate:

1. **Non-negotiable**: if this claim were false, what would the vault's mission be? If the answer is "the same," it's not a postulate.
2. **Structural**: the claim shapes how the vault reads sources, frames concepts, builds curriculum phases. Not a specific fact that could be checked — a premise that determines how facts are read.
3. **Pre-inquiry**: the vault commits to this before engaging the literature, not as a conclusion after engagement.
4. **Defensible but not defended here**: you could defend the postulate elsewhere, but this file states it rather than argues it.

A good postulate list has 5–10 entries. Fewer than 5 usually means the vault's foundations aren't fully articulated. More than 10 usually means some entries are conclusions rather than priors.

## Maintenance

- **Postulates are rarely revised.** If an emerging curriculum or note conflicts with a postulate, the first question is whether the note is wrong, not whether the postulate needs revising. Postulates are walls, not preferences.
- **Adding a postulate**: only when a pattern across multiple notes and curriculum phases clearly requires a structural commitment that no existing postulate captures. Rare — expect 1–3 additions across the vault's first year, then stability.
- **Retiring a postulate**: if sustained inquiry shows a postulate is wrong, retire it. Move to §Retired below with a dated note explaining the retirement. Do not silently delete — the retirement is itself a teaching moment for future students of the vault.
- **The vault's `vault-config.md` Q7 (output layer) should be consistent with the postulates.** If the vault's terminal output contradicts a postulate, one of them is incoherent.
